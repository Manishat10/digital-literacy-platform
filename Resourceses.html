Digital Literacy for Children (Age group 5-12)


Basic understanding of computers and devices: Introduce children to different types of computers, tablets, and smartphones, and teach them how to navigate these devices.

Online safety and privacy: Teach them about the importance of not sharing personal information online, avoiding strangers, and understanding the basics of online safety.

Introduction to coding: Provide basic coding exercises to familiarize them with programming concepts in a fun and interactive way

Digital Literacy for Teens (Age group 13-17)


Online privacy and security: Educate teenagers about the risks associated with sharing personal information online and how to protect themselves from cyber threats.

Media literacy: Teach them to critically evaluate online information, identify fake news, and understand the impact of media on society.

Social media management: Discuss the responsible use of social media, digital footprints, and how to build a positive online presence

Digital Literacy for Young Adults (Age group 18-25)


Digital professional skills: Teach them how to create a strong online resume, build a professional network, and use online job portals.

Digital financial literacy: Introduce them to online banking, budgeting tools, and secure online payment methods.

Online research skills: Enhance their research capabilities, fact-checking, and effective use of databases for academic or professional purposes.

Digital wellness: Discuss the importance of maintaining a healthy balance between online and offline activities and strategies for managing screen time

Digital Literacy for Adults (Age group 26+)


Advanced online security: Train adults to recognize and prevent online scams, phishing attacks, and identity
Digital citizenship: Discuss the responsibilities and rights of individuals in the digital age, including promoting positive online behavior and contributing to a safe digital community.
E-commerce and online shopping: Teach adults how to shop safely online, compare prices, and use secure payment methods.
Remote work and collaboration tools: Familiarize them with various online communication and collaboration tools used in remote work settings.
Media and information literacy: Help adults critically analyze information from various online sources and distinguish between reliable and unreliable content.
